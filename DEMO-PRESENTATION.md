## 🎨 Visual Design Guidelines for All Slides

### **Color Palette:**
- **Primary:** Deep blue (#1a1f3a) and electric blue (#00a8ff)
- **Accent:** Gold/amber (#f59e0b) - for Noviq branding
- **Danger:** Red (#ef4444)
- **Success:** Green (#10b981)
- **Background:** Dark navy or clean white (consistency across slides)

### **Typography:**
- **Headline:** Bold, sans-serif, 44-54pt
- **Body:** Clean sans-serif, 24-32pt
- **Emphasis:** Use color/weight, not too many fonts

### **Icon Style:**
- Modern, minimalist line icons
- Consistent weight and style across slides
- Use color to indicate status (red=bad, green=good, blue=neutral)

### **Layout:**
- Generous white space
- Visual hierarchy clear
- Not too much text per slide (judges should listen to you, not read)

## 📝 Speaker Notes & Tips

### **Delivery Guidelines:**

1. **Pace:** Speak clearly but confidently. Don't rush—34 seconds for 4 slides is plenty.

2. **Energy:** Start high energy on the hook, maintain through problem, shift to confident/calm for solution.

3. **Eye Contact:** Look at camera/judges when speaking, glance at slides to point, back to judges.

4. **Hand Gestures:** Use them to emphasize key points:
   - Point to attacker when saying "malicious prompt"
   - Shield gesture when saying "on-chain enforcement"
   - Checkmark gesture when showing successful block

5. **Transitions:** Smooth transitions between slides:
   - Hook → "So what's the problem?"
   - Problem → "Current solutions don't work because..."
   - Problem → Solution: "That's why we built Noviq."

6. **Demo Confidence:** Practice the live demo 10 times. Have backup screenshots ready in case of technical issues.

## 🎬 Final Tips

### **What Makes a Great Demo:**
- ✅ Clear problem statement (judges nod "yes, this is a real issue")
- ✅ Simple explanation (non-technical judges can follow)
- ✅ "Wow" moment (the attack fails dramatically)
- ✅ Clear differentiation (why this is better than alternatives)
- ✅ Confidence (you know your product inside-out)

### **What to Avoid:**
- ❌ Too much technical jargon
- ❌ Apologizing for anything ("sorry this is slow...")
- ❌ Reading slides word-for-word
- ❌ Rushing through the demo
- ❌ Not explaining what judges are seeing

### **If Something Goes Wrong:**
- Stay calm
- Have a backup ready
- Explain what *should* happen
- Move forward confidently
